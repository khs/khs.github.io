Dark band: ±1σ (68% interval)  |  Light band: ±2σ (95% interval). Bands are asymmetric: downside uses 50% annualised vol vs 28% upside, reflecting that oil crashes faster than it rallies (2008, 2020). Uncertainty plateaus past ~2.5 years due to long-run mean reversion. Calibrated from 20-year WTI backtest (EIA RCLC1–4 vs EIA spot).

Each line is a weekly snapshot of the forward curve at that date. Older snapshots appear lighter. As the dataset grows, you will be able to compare past predictions against realised prices.

Band calibration — did the predicted ranges hold up?

For every historical week, the model computed asymmetric uncertainty bands (downside vol 50%, upside 28%) around the futures price. This shows what fraction of realized prices actually fell inside those bands. Well-calibrated bands should hit ~68% and ~95%.

Point forecast accuracy — WTI, 2004–present

Prediction = EIA NYMEX nth-nearby futures price on date t. Realized = WTI spot price (EIA) at t + h. Naive = today's spot used as forecast (random-walk benchmark). A positive bias means futures under-predicted realized prices on average.

Monthly prediction error over time (1-month horizon)

Annual average absolute error by horizon

MAE (mean absolute error) in $/bbl per year. Years with large errors correspond to price dislocations: 2008 financial crisis, 2014–16 oil crash, 2020 COVID.
